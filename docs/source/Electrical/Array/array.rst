Panel Lamination Guide:

General:
    Keep Everything increadibly clean, especially the front film. Even small particles can ruin the whole process.


Specific Instructions:

    1. Solder Panels Together
        - Ensure they are parallel with a small space between them
        - Also Tape them with kapton tape
    2. Cut all bagging materials
        - 1 bag per panel approx 1 inch larger on all sides
        - 1 felt per bag approx 1-2 inches larger than the panel in all direction
        - 1 peel ply per panel approc 2 inches bigger than the panel in all directions
        - 1 bag per panel (some exceptions for larger ones) approc 4 inches bigger in all directitons
            - You can also wait here until placing the seam tape and measure to that instead.
    3. Clean surface for bagging
    4. Gather white boards for flippping and make sure they're all clean
    5. Get smaller bag onto the back of the panel and flip
    6. Mix resin
    7. Pour resin onto the front aof the panels and set the front film onto it.
    8. Squeege out the excess resin an bubbles form the center outwards(be careful with cracking the panels here if there are any elevation changes(there shoulden't be but if there are be carefu with them))
    9. Flip over and remove the bag
    10. Sodler the copper tabs on
    11. Cut slits in the fliberglass to allow the copper tabs to fit through
    12. Apply the first fiberglass and wet it out with resin until it is semi clear, remove all bubbles
    13. repeat steps 11 and 12 for the second fiberglass layer
    14. Apply fel peel ply and smooth it out
    15. Apply felt layer
    16. On the perimeterof the panel, add yellow seam tape and leave the backing(white tape stuff) on until ready to stick it to the bag
        - Remove the backijng gradually to prevent early contact with bag (early contact can cause air leaks)
    17. Set the bag onto the panel
    18. Get a scrap of felt, roll it up , and placce it under the bag wher ethe felt is touchin itself to ensure proper air flow
    19. Peel up the seam tape seciotns and stick it to the bag while keepign the bag taut (also but based of nozzle in before hand here)
    20. Add pleats
    21. Cut a small slitin the bag for the nozzle to slot in with the base
    22. Twist the nozzle to lock it
    23. Plug in the vaccum pump (its by the welding table with white plugs)
    24. Seal any holes or gas in the bag by pushign down on the seam table until air whistles stop.
